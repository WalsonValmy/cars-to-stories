Wendell Scott

The 1st Black driver to win a NASCAR Cup Series race.

Wendell Scott was a pioneering NASCAR driver and mechanic who made history as the first Black driver to win a NASCAR Cup Series race, earning his victory in 1963 at Jacksonville Speedway Park. Competing during the era of segregation, he faced intense racism, limited funding, and unequal treatment, yet still proved himself through talent and persistence. Scott’s legacy is one of resilience and courage, and he was posthumously inducted into the NASCAR Hall of Fame in 2015, cementing his place as a trailblazer in American motorsports.
